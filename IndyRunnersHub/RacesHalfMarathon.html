Half Marathon Race

Half Marathon Race Description

The half marathon is what many consider the most popular race distance. According to Running USA, 2 million runners did a half marathon last year. If you're considering running a marathon, running a half marathon is a great lead up.

For begining runners or runners who have not run farther than an 10K, the half marathon distance is certainly attainable. It is a challenging distance, but does not require the same committment that a full marathon requires.

Half Marathon Race Training Plan

Following a training plan can increase the likelihood of completing your goal and also motivate and prepare you for upcoming the upcoming race. For beginners, before you begin any training plan, the first thing you should do is see your doctor for a physical to make sure your body is ready for the upcoming training. The doctor can also give you an indication of how to integrate training safely.

The next step is to make sure you have the appropriate gear in reference to shoes and clothing. There are many options for different running shoes so you should get assistance to select a pair that is best for you. A good idea would be to visit a running store to find appropriate running shoes and clothing, particularly clothing with some moisture wicking fabric.

IndyRunnersHub has training plans designed by our coaches for the Half Marathon race for both the beginning and experienced runner. A good estimate of the time needed to prepare for this distance would be about twelve to eighteen weeks long

Half Marathon Race Nutrition

One of the biggest factors that will determine how you finish a half marathon is how you fuel your body before, during, and after the race, as well as during training. Nutrition and hydration when running can be the difference between barely finishing a race and finishing the race strong. And while fueling for a half marathon is not as intricate as fueling for a marathon, you still want to go in with a plan! Nutrition for half marathon training requires practice, trying different fueling options and alternatives, and timing nutrition around your runs and long runs. The coaches at IndyRunnersHUB can build a nutrition plan that is right for you.

As for any distance race, test all nutrition such as gels or energy drinks during training (also clothing). Nothing new on race day!
