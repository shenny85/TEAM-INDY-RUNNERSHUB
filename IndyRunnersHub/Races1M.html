1M Race

1M Race Description

Whether you're a new runner building up to your first-ever mile, an experienced athlete training for a fast 1600 meters, or a middle-of-the-pack runner just looking to fit a little more movement in your day, there's a lot to love about this distance.

Since it is a short race, it is perfect for the beginner. However, if you're an experienced runner and your thinking this race is too easy, you couldn't be more wrong! The mile is the tricky middle ground of running. Not short enough to be a sprint, not long enough to be an endurance event. But you still have to go all in. Just not too all-in, too early, or you're gonna be walking. Pushing your speed for a whole mile, even practiced milers admit, just hurts!

1M Race Training Plan

Following a training plan can increase the likelihood of completing your goal and also motivate and prepare you for upcoming the upcoming race. For beginners, before you begin any training plan, the first thing you should do is see your doctor for a physical to make sure your body is ready for the upcoming training. The doctor can also give you an indication of how to integrate training safely.

The next step is to make sure you have the appropriate gear in reference to shoes and clothing. There are many options for different running shoes so you should get assistance to select a pair that is best for you. A good idea would be to visit a running store to find appropriate running shoes and clothing, particularly clothing with some moisture wicking fabric.

IndyRunnersHub has training plans designed by our coaches for the 1M race for both the beginning and experienced runner. A good estimate of the time needed to prepare for a 1M would be about six to eight weeks for beginners.

Race Nutrition

For nutrition you always want to make sure your body is hydrated and that it has the fuel it needs to complete the race. An energy gel a half hour before the race should be sufficient. Eat a normal meal 2-3 hours before, but you don't want to eat much right before the race as this is a hard effort race.

As for any distance race, test all nutrition such as gels or energy drinks during training (also clothing) Nothing new on race day!
