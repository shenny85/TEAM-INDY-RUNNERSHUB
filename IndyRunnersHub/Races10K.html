10K Race

10K Race Description

The 10K race is a long distance race that is 6.2 miles, just a little under half the distance that you would run for a half marathon. This is a great race for building speed, strength, and endurance. You should maintain appropriate running technique and pace during the run. This race is geared for the more experienced runner however beginners have been successful as well.

10K Race Training Plan

Following a training plan can increase the likelihood of completion and also motivate and prepare you for upcoming activity. Before you begin any training plan the first thing you should do is check in with your doctor because the last thing you want to do is worsen any existing conditions or injuries. The provider can also give you a good idea of how to start and how often to integrate training. The next step is to make sure you have the appropriate gear in reference to shoes and clothing. There are many options for different running shoes so you should get assistance to select one that is best for you. A good idea would be to check out a running shoe store and also some type of moisture wicking fabric to run in.

Before you begin training it is important that you warm up and also that you cool down after finishing. It is also good to mention that runners also need adequate sleep which helps for rest and recovery.

Race Nutrition

For nutrition we always want to make sure our body is hydrated and that it has the fuel it needs to complete the race. A 10K is definitely the race where you need to begin hydrating during the run if you have not already. We need to figure out what to use and how often to use it during training, so that there are no surprises on race day. The biggest mistake runners make is not eating or drinking enough before a run or during. A good rule to maintain adequate hydration is to drink 5 to 10 ounces every 15 to 20 minutes of running. With regard to fuel and fuel stores, our bodies need lots of good sources of carbohydrates, healthy fats and proteins. Of these the primary energy source is carbohydrates, so participants may increase their carbohydrate intake, known as “carb loading” in the days before the run. The night before a 10K do not try new foods.

On race day it is important to eat a breakfast of mostly carbs and some protein. About 15 minutes before the 10K starts a high carbohydrate snack is necessary. We will utilize water and sports drinks for hydration to replace the electrolytes lost through sweat. After about 60 minutes runners will need additional fuel sources to replenish stored glycogen loss, so this is the perfect time to use sports gels or energy chews, or to eat simple bananas and raisins. As the run approaches 90 minutes or more this is the time that we will need to replace electrolytes with sports drink or convenient salt tablets. The goal is to have these essentials when we need them so practice during training will give you the optimal times to perform these tasks. After the race you can begin the recovery process. The key is to hydrate and eat a small snack immediately after the race.
